Rock Kit Contents

Basalt

Type: Igneous (Extrusive)

Location: Baraoas Rock Formations

Properties: Dark-colored, fine-grained, dense

Description: Basalt forms when lava cools rapidly on Earth’s surface. It is one of the most common volcanic rocks found in oceanic crust. Its dark color comes from iron- and magnesium-rich minerals.

Quartzite

Type: Metamorphic

Location: Kapurpurawan Rock Formations

Properties: Very hard, crystalline texture, resistant to weathering

Description: Quartzite forms when sandstone is exposed to intense heat and pressure. The quartz grains recrystallize and become tightly packed together. This process makes the rock extremely hard and durable.

Schist

Type: Metamorphic

Location: Baraoas Rock Formations

Properties: Layered texture, shiny minerals, medium to coarse-grained

Description: Schist is formed through the metamorphism of shale or mudstone. It contains visible mineral grains that give the rock a shiny appearance. The rock commonly shows layered or foliated textures due to pressure during formation.

Marble

Type: Metamorphic

Location: Baraoas Rock Formations

Properties: Smooth texture, crystalline, reacts with acid

Description: Marble forms when limestone undergoes heat and pressure deep underground. The calcite minerals recrystallize, giving the rock a smooth and crystalline appearance. Marble is commonly used in sculptures and construction materials.

Dead Coral Stone

Type: Sedimentary

Location: Kapurpurawan Rock Formations

Properties: Porous, lightweight, contains coral fragments

Description: Dead Coral Stone is formed from the remains of ancient coral organisms. Over time, the coral fragments become compacted and cemented together. The rock is commonly found in coastal and marine environments.

Conglomerate

Type: Sedimentary

Location: Currimao Rock Formations

Properties: Rounded gravel-sized clasts, coarse-grained

Description: Conglomerate is made up of rounded rock fragments cemented together by finer materials. The rounded shape of the clasts indicates they were transported by water. It commonly forms in riverbeds and high-energy environments.

Porphyiritic Dacite

Type: Igneous (Extrusive)

Location: Kapurpurawan Rock Formations

Properties: Fine-grained matrix with large crystals

Description: Porphyritic Dacite forms from volcanic magma that cools at different rates. Large crystals form first beneath the surface before the remaining magma erupts and cools quickly. This creates a rock with large mineral crystals embedded in a fine-grained groundmass.

Pumice

Type: Igneous (Extrusive)

Location: Baraoas Rock Formations

Properties: Lightweight, porous, rough texture

Description: Pumice forms during explosive volcanic eruptions. Gas bubbles become trapped as the lava cools rapidly, creating many holes in the rock. Because of its high porosity, pumice is light enough to float on water.

Sandstone

Type: Sedimentary

Location: Baraoas Rock Formations

Properties: Sandy texture, porous, layered appearance

Description: Sandstone forms from compacted and cemented sand grains over time. It is commonly deposited in deserts, beaches, and river environments. The rock often displays visible layers due to sediment deposition.

Amazonite

Type: Mineral (Feldspar)

Location: Currimao Rock Formations

Properties: Green-blue color, vitreous luster, hard

Description: Amazonite is a green variety of feldspar mineral known for its attractive color. It commonly forms in igneous rocks such as granite and pegmatite. The mineral is often used as a gemstone and decorative material.

Gabbro

Type: Igneous (Intrusive)

Location: Currimao Rock Formations

Properties: Dark-colored, coarse-grained, dense

Description: Gabbro forms when magma cools slowly beneath Earth’s surface. The slow cooling process allows large mineral crystals to develop. It is compositionally similar to basalt but has a coarser texture.

Rhyolite

Type: Igneous (Extrusive)

Location: Currimao Rock Formations

Properties: Light-colored, fine-grained, silica-rich

Description: Rhyolite forms from silica-rich lava that cools rapidly after volcanic eruptions. It is usually light in color due to its high quartz and feldspar content. Some rhyolite rocks contain visible crystals formed before eruption.

Tuff

Type: Igneous (Pyroclastic)

Location: Kapurpurawan Rock Formations

Properties: Lightweight, porous, composed of volcanic ash

Description: Tuff forms from volcanic ash and debris ejected during eruptions. Over time, the ash particles become compacted and cemented together. The rock is commonly associated with volcanic regions.

Hydrozincite

Type: Mineral

Location: Currimao Rock Formations

Properties: White color, soft, chalky texture

Description: Hydrozincite is a secondary zinc carbonate mineral formed by weathering processes. It commonly appears as white crusts or coatings on zinc-rich rocks. The mineral is relatively soft and has a chalky texture.

Andesite

Type: Igneous (Extrusive)

Location: Baraoas Rock Formations

Properties: Fine-grained, grayish color, intermediate composition

Description: Andesite forms from volcanic lava with intermediate silica content. It commonly develops in volcanic arcs associated with tectonic plate boundaries. The rock usually has a fine-grained texture due to rapid cooling.

Granodiorite

Type: Igneous (Intrusive)

Location: Currimao Rock Formations

Properties: Coarse-grained, light-colored, durable

Description: Granodiorite forms from slowly cooled magma beneath Earth’s surface. Its coarse texture is caused by the growth of large mineral crystals during cooling. The rock is commonly used as a construction and decorative material.

Pebble

Type: Sedimentary Clast

Location: Baraoas Rock Formations

Properties: Rounded, smooth surface, small size

Description: Pebbles are small rock fragments shaped by erosion and water transport. Continuous movement in rivers and beaches smoothens their surfaces over time. They are commonly found in sedimentary deposits and riverbeds.

Dacite

Type: Igneous (Extrusive)

Location: Kapurpurawan Rock Formations

Properties: Fine-grained, light to medium gray, silica-rich

Description: Dacite forms from volcanic magma with relatively high silica content. It commonly erupts from stratovolcanoes and cools quickly on the surface. The rock may contain small visible crystals within a fine-grained matrix.

Bioclastic Limestone

Type: Sedimentary

Location: Kapurpurawan Rock Formations

Properties: Contains shell fragments, porous, reacts with acid

Description: Bioclastic Limestone forms from accumulated shell fragments and other biological debris. Over time, these materials become compacted and cemented together. The rock is commonly found in shallow marine environments.

Coraline Limestone

Type: Sedimentary

Location: Kapurpurawan Rock Formations

Properties: Porous, fossil-rich, light-colored

Description: Coralline Limestone forms mainly from coral remains and marine organisms. The accumulated materials become lithified over long periods of time. This rock is commonly associated with coral reef environments and coastal areas.
